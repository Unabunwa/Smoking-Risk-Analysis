# 📊 Health Insights: Smoking, BMI & Kidney Health

This directory highlights key insights the dataset, focusing on how **smoking behavior, age, gender, and BMI** relate to potential kidney health risks.

---

## 🔍 Key Insights

### A. Smoking Profile
- 47.3% never smoked; 52.7% are current/former smokers  
- **Insight:** Tobacco exposure is the majority experience in this population  

---

### B. Smoking vs. Age
- Smoking dips in midlife (39–48), then rises sharply  
- Highest risk: **59–68 age group** (high intensity + long duration)  
- **Insight:** Risk accumulates with age  

---

### C. Weight–Gender Paradox
- Males dominate **underweight and obese** categories  
- **Insight:** Gender disparity in extreme BMI and smoking risk  

---

### D. BMI & Age Interaction
- Obesity is most prevalent across age groups  
- Strong link between **obesity and heavy smoking**  
- **Insight:** Risk factors are often comorbid  

---

## ⚠️ Takeaways
- Smoking is highly prevalent  
- Risk increases with age  
- Males show higher exposure to extreme risk factors  
- Obesity and smoking frequently occur together
